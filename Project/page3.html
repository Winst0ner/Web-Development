Cody


Description

A cody is a one and a quarter backflip from your stomach.


Timing

Timing a cody is the most difficult part of the trick. Codys are relativly easy tricks but they are very sketchy when you are still perfecting the timing. To do a cody, you land on your stomach with your legs in a 90 degree angle. Right as you are about to leave the trampoline, you push against the trampoline with your arms and swing your feet down to get backwards rotation.


Rotation

Codys are harder than backflips because you have farther to rotate to get over your head and becuase getting good rotation is not easy. In a backflip you can get good rotation every time but with a cody, sometimes you just don't get enough. If your feet hit the tramp when they swing down then it will steal all of your rotation. Sometimes you won't get enough rotation because your timeing is off but there are ways to get extra rotation. If you do a backflip to stomach before the cody, you can transfer the rotation from the backflip into the cody. This is great for getting extra rotation but it does make the cody a little harder to commit to because you have to have a good set.


Trampoline Terms

Rotation
A rotation is a full spin in the forwards or backwards direction.
Twisting
Twisting is spinning in a 180 or 360 during a flip.
Tucking
Tucking is pulling your legs into yourself to rotate faster.
