Fliffus


Description

A fliffus is a double frontflip with a half twist in the second flip.


Air Awareness

Air awareness is one of the most important skills you learn in trampolining. It is being able to tell where you are in the flip and how long till you land. Without air awareness it is impossible to do any trick bigger that a single flip. To progress to double flips, you have to be able to tell which flip you are on and which direction you are facing. Without it, you would never know when to open up or when to twist.


Twisting

Once you have the air awareness to see the floor inbetween your first frontflip and the second, you can start adding the twist into the second flip. As soon as you see the ground inbetween the flips, you open up partially and do a front half. Doing this front half will make spotting your landing much easier so you can ajust your rotation accordingly.


Trampoline Terms

Rotation
A rotation is a full spin in the forwards or backwards direction.
Twisting
Twisting is spinning in a 180 or 360 during a flip.
Tucking
Tucking is pulling your legs into yourself to rotate faster.
